Similarity

Historical Identity: Both belong to the Kowloon Thirteen Villages with origins tracing back centuries (Yuan/Ming/Qing). Vital clan settlements before urbanisation.
External Pressures: Both faced disruption during Japanese Occupation (airport expansion) and post-war squatter clearances (1950s-60s).
2019 Policy: Both identified for accelerated public housing development to address Hong Kong's land supply crisis.

URA-led Revitalisation

  • Classic Walled Village: Defensive layout, intact gatehouse/temple, central axis
  • Grade III Protection: Mandatory Archaeological Impact Assessment triggered
  • Outcome: High-rise towers around 2,000m² Conservation Park (in-situ)

Government Clearance

  • Kangxi Era (1683-84): Lin clan branched to Pu Gang (demolished Japanese Occupation)
  • 1957 Catastrophe: 480 people forcibly relocated to Lok Fu estates; NO walls, gatehouse, ancestral hall, central axis left
  • Current State: Only dozen ordinary stone houses remain near Wong Tai Sin Temple
  • Zero AAB Grading: Confirmed ungraded (unlike nearby Grade I Temple)
  • 2023-2026: Hong Kong Housing Society direct resumption; site formation ongoing for 700+ units

Differences

  • Architecture: Nga Tsin Wai: Complete walled structure vs Chuk Yuen: No original architecture (X walls, X gatehouse, X ancestral hall)
  • 1957 Impact: Chuk Yuen lost 90%+ area/population vs Nga Tsin Wai retained core
  • Statutory: Cap. 53 Grade III vs Zero AAB grading
  • Institution: URA profit-balancing vs Housing Society unit-maximisation
  • Resistance: Post-2006 heritage movement vs 1957 pre-advocacy trauma (+recent FB/wiki but no traction)
